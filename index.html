Why with increasing temperature the voltage decreases?

It's a common thing to think about the relationship between E(cell) and temperature since it’s an experiment in the physical chemistry lab, so what’s happening in this experiment and why?

In this experiment, you have to prepare daniel’s cell ( galvanic cell ) or build it and it’s simple to do that grab two beakers then cathode and anode, so in our cell, it will be ( copper and zinc ) respectively and put them in the beakers where we already put them there ions solutions and a salt bridge (KCL ) between them ( CuSo4, ZnSo4 ) , ( Cu+2, Zn+2 ) then connect both the cathode and anode with a the wire then put a galvanometer on the wire to measure the electromotive force ( EMF ) or E(cell) between the cathode and anode while the solutions beakers in a thermostat that increase the temperature on the cell solutions with time and see what happens to the E(cell) values.

Daniel’s cell ( galvanic cell ):

After the experimental measurements on our cell, we can plot them in the diagram and we will have these results:

As we see from the diagram the slope in the equation has a negative value so now we know that it’s an inverse relation between E(cell) and T (kᵒ).

So now we know how to build a galvanic cell and what kind of relations between E(cell) and T (kᵒ) so now we ask ourselves why is it inverse?

First, from experiment values we can find Gibbs free energy and enthalpy using these equations : Gibbs free energy: ∆G=-n F E Where :

n: Number of moles
F: Faraday’s constant
E: Electromotive force

Enthalpy: ∆H = -nFE-nFT (-∆E/∆T) Slope of diagram = ∆E/∆T

And in the end, we will rise the temperature and get a negative value for both of them and now we should know that our reaction is (spontaneous and exothermic) so with increasing temperature our reaction will shift to lift towards the reactant in our oxidation-reduction reaction so the reaction won’t go well and it will reduce the electromotive force in our galvanic cell.

And now let’s see the reason why it’s an inverse relation between EMF and T(kᵒ) at the ionic or atomic level and you can say that the reason is:

Because in our Galvanic cell we got zinc and copper they connected to each other with a wire so the electrons move from the anode (Zinc) to cathode (copper) because copper pulls electrons more than zinc so it takes electrons from zinc by the connected wire between them and that's the electricity (movement of electrons) so when electrons go to copper metal, the copper ions will be in a copper sulfate solution so we got a free ion of copper (Cu+2) in our solution and when these electrons go to copper metal it gives them to the copper ions in the solution and become neutral so it sticks to copper metal it will increase the size (notice the size difference of the cathode between the beginning and the end of the experiment) and that happens in the room temperature (normal case) but when we increase the temperature of the cell solutions and as we know that our reaction is spontaneous and exothermic from the entropy and enthalpy experimental values (negative values) so now we know that's the energy that came from temperature goes to the ions in the solution and it gains a spontaneous movement energy a (kinetic energy) and start to hit the walls of the container away from the metal so our ions became two parts, one that gains energy and move away from metal so it will be distributed from it’s the main job (take electrons from metal and become a neutral) and it doesn’t take any electrons from the metal, and the other goes towards metal and gains electrons to become neutral and continue the electron movement between the cathode and anode so with increasing temperature the number of ions that goes away from metal is increasing and the usage of electrons than have been pulled from zinc to copper metal will decrease because the copper metal starts to become richer than zinc electrons that have been pulled and it will decrease the pull of electrons so the motion of electrons in the wire will decrease and the voltage will decrease as increasing of temperature because the voltage E(cell) is the electron movement in the wire.
